# Intelligence Engineering Methodology

- Start with intelligence requirements: what decision, uncertainty, or hypothesis must the analysis support?
- Separate observed fact, source claim, inference, assumption, hypothesis, unknown, and gap.
- Model entities by identity, ownership, function, relationship, exposure, trust boundary, business relevance, and confidence.
- Expand assets only from traceable evidence: domains, subdomains, applications, infrastructure, repositories, accounts, people, vendors, documents, and process dependencies.
- Prefer traceable evidence chains over large unqualified inventories.
- Every claim needs source, observation time, observed fact, inference, and confidence.
- Evaluate sources by proximity, freshness, authority, consistency, and conflict with other evidence.
- Confidence labels: confirmed, likely, possible, unknown; never hide uncertainty.
- Absence of evidence is a gap, not proof of absence.
- Hypotheses must state target, relationship context, evidence, suspected weakness, disproof condition, scope limit, and expected validation signal.
- Do not infer exploitability, compromise, business impact, or attribution from indirect evidence alone.
- Prioritize analysis by scope certainty, evidence strength, relevance, validation cost, and operational risk.
